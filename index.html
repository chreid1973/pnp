The Manifesto

1. Potatoes

Potatoes are ideas. They’re raw, flexible, comforting, infinite. You can mash them, fry them, hold them up to the light and go, “I see a shape.” But nobody landed a spaceship on mashed potatoes.

2. Towers

Products are towers. They are chosen, finished, and built so someone else can use them. They have doors, stairs, docs, and a clear purpose. They don’t exist for you, they exist for the next human.

3. Meaning is in construction

Nothing matters until it’s in the world. Drafts are a vibe. Shipping is a worldview. We celebrate finishers.

4. This line is a filter

“Potatoes ≠ Product” is how builders spot each other in the wild. Wear it to nudge the half-finished geniuses in your orbit.

Where this came from

Someone said, “This ain’t no Devil’s Tower made out of mashed potatoes,” and an entire creative philosophy fell out of the sky. It was about refusing to stay in the proto-idea phase. It was about honoring craft over vibes. It was about making the landing pad, not just imagining the UFO.

This store is a shrine to that energy. One foot in sci‑fi, one foot in the workshop, both eyes on the shipping lane.
